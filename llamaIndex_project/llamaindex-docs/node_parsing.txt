In LlamaIndex, Node Parsing is the process of breaking down larger documents into smaller, manageable chunks called Nodes.
Since Large Language Models have context window limits, you cannot usually feed an entire book or a large PDF into the model at once.
A Node Parser splits the text based on token limits, sentences, or paragraphs, while preserving important metadata.
LlamaIndex provides various parsers, such as the SentenceSplitter, which ensures that sentences are not broken in the middle.
These nodes are then converted into embeddings and stored in the vector database.
