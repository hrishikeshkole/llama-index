Embeddings are numerical representations of real-world objects that machine learning algorithms can process.
When applied to text, an embedding model maps words, sentences, or entire documents into a high-dimensional vector space.
The key property of embeddings is that texts with similar meanings are mapped to vectors that are close to each other in this space.
This mathematical proximity allows search engines and RAG systems to find relevant information based on semantic meaning rather than just exact keyword matching.
For example, the words "dog" and "puppy" will have very similar embedding vectors.
